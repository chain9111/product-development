## Quality Control Requirements

### Incoming Inspection
- Connector dimensional verification
- Cable electrical properties testing
- Visual inspection for defects
- Certification documentation review

### In-Process Inspection
- Soldering quality (IPC-A-610 Class 2)
- Strain relief installation
- Cable assembly workmanship
- Marking and labeling verification

### Final Inspection (100% Testing)
- Continuity test (all conductors)
- Hi-pot test (1500V AC)
- Insulation resistance test
- Pull force test (sample, per lot)
- Visual inspection
- Functional test (mate/unmate)

### Critical Quality Points
1. Solder joints (connector pins)
2. Strain relief installation
3. Shield termination
4. Connector orientation/keying
5. Cable jacket integrity

## Assembly Notes

### Special Tools Required
- Soldering station (temperature controlled)
- Heat gun for heat shrink
- Wire strippers (18 AWG)
- Crimp tool (if applicable)
- Multimeter for testing
- Hi-pot tester (production)

### Assembly Time
- Cable prep and strip: 3 minutes
- Connector soldering (×2): 8 minutes
- Strain relief installation: 2 minutes
- Heat shrink application: 1 minute
- Testing and inspection: 1 minute
- **Total:** 15 minutes per unit

### Skill Level
- Operator skill: Intermediate
- Soldering certification: IPC J-STD-001 preferred
- Training time: 2-4 hours
